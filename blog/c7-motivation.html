The art of self-motivation

Cultural 7 | Date: 6/5/2016
How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

I am co-founder of a educational company that helps children meditate and learn more about themselves. We are helping the world develop its children so that they can be "happy, healthy, prosperous and connected". I also trade currencies with my other co-founder friends and family. We travel the world, learn and devote our 9-5 time to helping people empower themselves through self-reflective education.

How do you feel?

I feel "happy, healthy, prosperous and connected". I am at peace, happy and energised by my decisions.

What do people say about you?

He is energised because he is optimistic and happy. He values his time and is self-directed. He is attentive and listens, he is decisive and disciplined. He learns constantly. He is happy. He values purpose over pleasure.

My core values are…

• Open mindness
• Curiosity
• Being Nice
• Being Happy
• Learning
• Understanding
• Logic and Skepticism

I stand for…

• Animals / Life
• Scientific enquiry
• Material, idealogical and spiritual freedom
• Humour
• Individual freedom
• Quality of Life
• Cause and effect
• Sustainability
• Empathy and understanding

Here are some quotes I found useful...

“My own dream, for example, is to create the conditions for world peace in my lifetime. I envision a world that is peaceful because inner peace, inner joy, and compassion are widespread, and those qualities are widespread as a consequence of ancient wisdom practices being made accessible to the modern world. I envision myself as a person who makes wisdom practices accessible by making them understandable, practical, and useful in the corporate world and beyond. When I started thinking about this, I knew my goal was impossible, but I talked about it to a lot of people anyway. The more I talked about it, the more it went from being impossible to implausible, and then from implausible to possible, and more importantly, it went from possible to actionable. I reached a state in my mind in which I felt there were actually things I could do to move it forward”

“The second important benefit is the more you talk to people about your ideal future, the more likely you can find people to help you. This is especially true if your aspiration for the future is altruistic in nature because people will rush to help you.”

Excerpt From: Tan, Chade-Meng. “Search Inside Yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness (and World Peace).”
